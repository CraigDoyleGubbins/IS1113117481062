Snapchat

Snapchat is an Android and iOS social media application, where users send and recieve photographs that disappear soon after being viewed. Snap - Snapchat's parent company - has a $2-billion 5-year contract with Google. This makes them Google's largest customer of their cloud platform. Snapchat may be making use of Google's Cloud Storage, App Engine, Compute Engine, etc.

Facebook

As the world's largest social media platform, with over 2 billion monthly active users and several mobile and web applications, it's easier for Facebook to have its own cloud servers. They have several datacenters in the US and one in Sweden. Here, they store photos, posts, conversations, etc and they mine data.

Netflix

Netflix relies on Amazon Web Services (AWS) for all of its scalable computing and storage needs - their business logic, user analytics, recommendations, etc. that make up the Netflix application, along with their huge selection of on-demand movies and TV series. Netflix took 8 years to migrate to the cloud as they had to rebuild their systems in order to do so.

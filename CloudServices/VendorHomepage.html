Software as a Service

Software as a Service (SaaS) is a software licensing and delivery model in which software is licensed on a subscription basis and is centrally hosted. SaaS is typically accessed by users using a thin client via a web browser. SaaS has become a common delivery model for many business applications, including office software, messaging software, payroll processing software, etc. Salesforce is an example of a leading provider of SaaS.

Salesforce

Platform as a Service

Platforms as a Service (PaaS) are cloud computing services that provide a platform allowing customers to develop, run, and manage applications without the complexity of building and maintaining the infrastructure typically associated with developing and launching an app. They let you work on projects from your office, home, or anywhere using the internet. Cloud9 is a cloud-based IDE that lets you write, run, and debug your code in your browser.

Cloud9

Infrastructure as a Service

Infrastructures as a Service (IaaS) are online services that provide infrastructure like physical computing resources, scaling, security, backup etc. to customers who find it is easier, faster and more cost-efficient to operate a workload without having to buy, manage and support the required infrastructure. This is an effective model for workloads that are temporary, experimental or that change unexpectedly. AWS is a dominant Iaas provider.

AWS

Communication as a Service

Communications as a Service (CaaS) is a collection of different vendor services that facilitate business communications. As with other cloud services, organizations may use these services to lower costs and increase efficiency for business processes involving text, audio or video telecommunications, as the service provider is responsible for the hardware and software management. Gmail is an example of a Caas provider.

Gmail
